Building a "Value-First" AI Product

Using Lovable.dev, we'll build an intelligent app that provides value *before* asking users to sign up, creating a truly delightful experience.

The Old Way: High Friction, Low Value

Most apps force users through tedious sign-up forms before they see any value. This leads to high drop-off rates and frustrated users.

Our Solution: The "Value-First" Funnel

We'll flip the model: **Engage** with an interactive tool, **Personalize** the experience with AI, and then **Convert** the user by offering to save their results.

The App Architecture

This is how our components work together. A user interacts with the frontend, which communicates with our backend API. The API uses an AI model and a database to deliver personalized results.

View on Eraser

Step 1: The Interactive Hook

First, we build the anonymous, multi-step questionnaire. It's a conversational UI that feels engaging and captures the user's goals without needing an account.

Step 2: The AI Reveal & Conversion

Next, we take the user's answers, get instant AI-powered career path recommendations, and then prompt them to create an account to save and explore their results.

Step 3: The Data Foundation

Now we set up our database. This ER diagram shows how we'll store user data, their profiles, and the vast amount of college and course information.

Step 4: AI Data Sourcing

Manually populating a database is slow. We'll use a prompt to have our AI model generate and insert thousands of records for courses and universities automatically.

Step 5: The Progressive Feedback Loop

Once a user is signed in, the app starts learning. Saving a course or university as a "favorite" refines their profile and leads to even smarter future recommendations.

Step 6: Monetization

Finally, we add a premium plan. With a simple prompt, we can integrate Stripe to unlock advanced features, creating a clear path to revenue.

Conclusion: Focus on the Idea

Lovable.dev automates the complex parts, letting you focus on creating delightful, intelligent products. The most valuable part of an application is the idea, not the code.
